# Carbohydrates

Carbohydrates are the body's preferred fuel source, especially for the brain and high-intensity exercise.

## Types
- **Simple carbs** — Quick-digesting sugars (glucose, fructose, sucrose). Found in fruit, honey, table sugar.
- **Complex carbs** — Longer chains that digest slowly, providing steady energy. Found in whole grains, legumes, starchy vegetables.
- **Fiber** — Indigestible carbs that support gut health and satiety. Soluble fiber (oats, beans) lowers cholesterol; insoluble fiber (whole wheat, vegetables) aids digestion.

## Daily Targets
- **General** — 45–65% of total calories (225–325 g on a 2000 kcal diet)
- **Endurance athletes** — 6–10 g/kg body weight
- **Low-carb approaches** — 50–150 g/day depending on the protocol

## Glycemic Index
- **Low GI (< 55)** — Lentils, most fruits, steel-cut oats. Steady blood sugar.
- **Medium GI (56–69)** — Brown rice, whole wheat bread, sweet potato.
- **High GI (> 70)** — White bread, white rice, potatoes. Fast energy but rapid blood sugar spike.

## Top Sources
Oats, brown rice, sweet potatoes, quinoa, beans, whole-grain bread, bananas, berries.
