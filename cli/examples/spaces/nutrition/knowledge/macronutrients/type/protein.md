# Protein

Protein is made up of amino acids — 9 of which are essential and must come from food.

## Daily Targets
- **Sedentary adults** — 0.8 g per kg of body weight
- **Active individuals** — 1.2–1.6 g/kg
- **Strength athletes** — 1.6–2.2 g/kg

## Complete vs Incomplete
- **Complete proteins** contain all 9 essential amino acids: meat, fish, eggs, dairy, soy, quinoa.
- **Incomplete proteins** lack one or more: beans, lentils, grains, nuts. Combine throughout the day to cover all amino acids.

## Top Sources (per 100 g)
| Food | Protein |
|------|---------|
| Chicken breast | 31 g |
| Greek yogurt | 10 g |
| Lentils (cooked) | 9 g |
| Eggs (2 large) | 12 g |
| Tofu (firm) | 17 g |

## Timing
Spreading protein across 3–4 meals (20–40 g each) maximizes muscle protein synthesis better than loading it into one meal.
