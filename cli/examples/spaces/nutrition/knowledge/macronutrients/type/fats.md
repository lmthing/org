# Fats

Dietary fat is calorie-dense (9 kcal/g vs 4 kcal/g for protein and carbs) but essential for hormone production, brain function, and absorbing fat-soluble vitamins (A, D, E, K).

## Types
- **Unsaturated (healthy)** — Monounsaturated (olive oil, avocado) and polyunsaturated (fish, walnuts, flaxseed). Lower inflammation and support heart health.
- **Saturated** — Solid at room temperature (butter, coconut oil, red meat fat). Limit to < 10% of calories.
- **Trans fats** — Industrially hydrogenated oils. Avoid entirely — linked to heart disease.
- **Omega-3 fatty acids** — EPA and DHA (fatty fish) and ALA (flaxseed, chia). Anti-inflammatory, critical for brain health. Aim for 2 servings of fatty fish per week.

## Daily Targets
- **General** — 20–35% of total calories (44–78 g on a 2000 kcal diet)
- **Minimum** — Do not go below 15% of calories; hormonal disruption can occur.

## Top Sources
Olive oil, avocado, salmon, sardines, walnuts, almonds, chia seeds, eggs, dark chocolate (70%+).
