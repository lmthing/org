# Calorie Tracking

A quantitative approach to nutrition — set daily calorie and macronutrient targets, then track intake against them.

## Setting Your Target
1. **Calculate TDEE** (Total Daily Energy Expenditure):
   - BMR (Basal Metabolic Rate) x Activity Multiplier
   - Sedentary: BMR x 1.2 | Lightly active: x 1.375 | Active: x 1.55 | Very active: x 1.725
2. **Adjust for goal**:
   - **Fat loss** — Subtract 300–500 kcal (moderate deficit)
   - **Maintenance** — Eat at TDEE
   - **Muscle gain** — Add 200–300 kcal (lean bulk)

## Macro Split (Starting Point)
- **Balanced** — 30% protein / 40% carbs / 30% fat
- **High protein** — 35% protein / 35% carbs / 30% fat
- **Endurance** — 25% protein / 50% carbs / 25% fat

## Practical Tips
- Weigh food raw (cooked weight varies)
- Front-load protein — plan protein sources first, fill in carbs and fats
- Don't aim for perfection — within 50–100 kcal of target is fine
- Track for 2–4 weeks to build intuition, then transition to intuitive eating if desired

## Common Pitfalls
- Forgetting cooking oils (1 tbsp = 120 kcal)
- Ignoring liquid calories (lattes, juice, alcohol)
- Weekend "cheat days" that erase the weekly deficit
- Obsessive tracking leading to disordered eating — take breaks if it feels unhealthy
