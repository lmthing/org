# Vitamin B12

A water-soluble vitamin critical for neurological function, red blood cell production, and DNA synthesis. Only naturally found in animal products.

## Functions
- Red blood cell formation (deficiency causes megaloblastic anemia)
- Myelin sheath maintenance (nerve protection)
- DNA synthesis and cellular energy production
- Homocysteine metabolism (cardiovascular protection)

## Daily Targets
- **Adults** — 2.4 mcg/day
- **Pregnant women** — 2.6 mcg/day
- **Breastfeeding** — 2.8 mcg/day

## At-Risk Groups
- **Vegans and vegetarians** — No reliable plant sources; supplementation essential
- **Adults over 50** — Reduced stomach acid impairs absorption from food
- **People on metformin or PPIs** — These medications reduce B12 absorption

## Sources
- **Animal foods** — Liver (70 mcg/serving), clams (84 mcg), salmon (4.8 mcg), eggs (1.1 mcg)
- **Fortified foods** — Nutritional yeast, plant milks, breakfast cereals
- **Supplements** — Cyanocobalamin or methylcobalamin (sublingual for better absorption)

## Deficiency Signs
Tingling/numbness in hands and feet, memory problems, fatigue, balance issues, glossitis (swollen tongue). Neurological damage can be irreversible if untreated.
