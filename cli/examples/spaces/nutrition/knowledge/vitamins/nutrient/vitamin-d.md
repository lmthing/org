# Vitamin D

A fat-soluble vitamin synthesized in the skin via UV-B sunlight exposure. Widespread deficiency, especially in northern latitudes.

## Functions
- Calcium absorption and bone mineralization
- Immune system modulation
- Mood regulation (linked to seasonal affective disorder)
- Muscle function

## Daily Targets
- **Adults** — 600–800 IU (15–20 mcg)
- **Deficient individuals** — 1000–4000 IU under medical supervision
- **Upper limit** — 4000 IU/day for adults

## Deficiency Signs
Fatigue, bone pain, muscle weakness, frequent illness, depression (especially in winter).

## Sources
- **Sunlight** — 10–30 minutes of midday sun on arms and face (varies by latitude and skin tone)
- **Fatty fish** — Salmon (570 IU/serving), mackerel, sardines
- **Fortified foods** — Milk, orange juice, cereals
- **Egg yolks** — ~40 IU per yolk
- **Supplements** — D3 (cholecalciferol) is better absorbed than D2
