# Iron

Iron is essential for hemoglobin (oxygen transport in blood) and myoglobin (oxygen storage in muscles). It also supports energy metabolism and immune function.

## Types
- **Heme iron** — Found in animal foods. Absorbed 2–3x more efficiently (15–35% absorption). Sources: red meat, poultry, shellfish.
- **Non-heme iron** — Found in plant foods. Lower absorption (2–20%). Sources: lentils, spinach, tofu, fortified cereals.

## Daily Targets
- **Men** — 8 mg/day
- **Women (19–50)** — 18 mg/day (higher due to menstruation)
- **Pregnant women** — 27 mg/day
- **Vegetarians** — 1.8x the standard recommendation (lower bioavailability)

## Absorption Tips
- **Enhancers** — Vitamin C dramatically boosts non-heme iron absorption. Pair iron-rich foods with citrus, bell peppers, or tomatoes.
- **Inhibitors** — Calcium, tannins (tea/coffee), and phytates (whole grains) reduce absorption. Separate from iron-rich meals by 1–2 hours.

## Deficiency Signs
Fatigue, pale skin, brittle nails, cold hands/feet, shortness of breath, restless legs.
