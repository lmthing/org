# Italian Cuisine

Italian cooking centers on high-quality, seasonal ingredients prepared simply to let flavors shine.

## Key Principles
- **Simplicity** — Most dishes use 4–8 ingredients. The quality of each ingredient matters more than complexity.
- **Regionality** — Northern Italy favors butter, rice, and polenta; Southern Italy uses olive oil, tomatoes, and dried pasta.
- **Al dente** — Pasta should be cooked firm to the bite. Start tasting 2 minutes before the package time.
- **Soffritto** — The holy trinity of onion, carrot, and celery, slowly sweated in olive oil, forms the base of countless dishes.

## Essential Techniques
- **Emulsifying pasta water** — Reserve starchy pasta water and toss with sauce to create a silky coating.
- **Risotto stirring** — Constant stirring releases starch for a creamy texture without cream.
- **Bruschetta method** — Rub toasted bread with raw garlic, then drizzle with good olive oil.

## Staple Ingredients
Olive oil, San Marzano tomatoes, Parmigiano-Reggiano, fresh basil, garlic, dried pasta, arborio rice, prosciutto, mozzarella.

## Classic Dishes
Cacio e pepe, carbonara, margherita pizza, risotto alla milanese, osso buco, tiramisu.
