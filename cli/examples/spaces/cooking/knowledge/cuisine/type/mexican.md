# Mexican Cuisine

Mexican cooking is one of the world's great culinary traditions — built on indigenous ingredients, bold chiles, and techniques refined over millennia.

## Key Principles
- **Chile complexity** — Different chiles serve different roles: ancho for sweetness, chipotle for smoke, habanero for heat. Toast dried chiles before rehydrating.
- **Nixtamalization** — Treating corn with lime (cal) transforms it into masa, the foundation of tortillas and tamales. This ancient technique unlocks niacin and changes the flavor profile.
- **Layered seasoning** — Flavors are built in stages: toast, char, blend, simmer.
- **Low and slow** — Barbacoa, birria, and carnitas all rely on long, gentle cooking to break down tough cuts.

## Essential Techniques
- **Salsa making** — Char tomatoes and chiles on a comal or under a broiler before blending. The char adds smoky depth.
- **Tortilla pressing** — Masa should be the texture of Play-Doh. Press thin, cook on a dry comal at high heat, and look for the puff.
- **Mole building** — Toast chiles, nuts, seeds, and spices separately, then blend and simmer for hours. Patience is the key ingredient.

## Staple Ingredients
Corn masa, dried chiles (ancho, guajillo, chipotle), black beans, tomatillos, cilantro, lime, avocado, queso fresco, epazote, Mexican oregano.

## Classic Dishes
Tacos al pastor, mole poblano, pozole, tamales, chiles rellenos, elote, churros.
