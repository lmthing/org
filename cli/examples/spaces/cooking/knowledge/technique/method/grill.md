# Grill

Grilling uses direct radiant heat from below to cook food quickly, creating characteristic char marks and smoky flavor.

## When to Use
- Steaks, burgers, chops, and other quick-cooking proteins
- Vegetables with enough structure to hold together (corn, zucchini, peppers, eggplant)
- Flatbreads, pizza, and fruit (peaches, pineapple)

## Key Technique
1. **Preheat thoroughly** — 15–20 minutes with the lid closed. The grates should be too hot to hold your hand near for more than 2 seconds.
2. **Clean and oil the grates** — Brush with a grill brush, then oil with a folded paper towel dipped in high-smoke-point oil.
3. **Two-zone setup** — Pile coals on one side (or turn on only half the burners) for a hot sear zone and a cooler indirect zone.
4. **Don't press** — Pressing burgers or chicken squeezes out juices. Let the food sit until it releases naturally.
5. **Rest the meat** — Rest for 5–10 minutes after grilling. Internal temperature will rise 3–5°F during resting.

## Temperature Guide
- **High (230°C+ / 450°F+)** — Steaks, thin cuts, vegetables
- **Medium (175–230°C / 350–450°F)** — Chicken pieces, fish, sausages
- **Low indirect (120–150°C / 250–300°F)** — Whole chickens, ribs, large roasts

## Common Mistakes
- Opening the lid too often (loses heat and extends cooking time)
- Only using direct heat (thick cuts burn outside before cooking through)
- Not letting the grill preheat (food sticks and doesn't char properly)
