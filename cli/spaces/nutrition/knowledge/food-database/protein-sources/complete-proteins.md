# Complete Protein Sources

Complete proteins contain all 9 essential amino acids in sufficient quantities.

## Animal Sources (per 100g cooked)
| Source | Protein | PDCAAS | Best For |
|--------|---------|--------|----------|
| Egg whites | 11g | 1.00 | Lean bulk, baking |
| Chicken breast | 31g | 0.95 | Lean meals, meal prep |
| Salmon | 20g | 0.94 | Omega-3, heart health |
| Greek yogurt | 10g | 1.00 | Snacks, breakfast |
| Whey protein | 80g | 1.00 | Post-workout |

## Plant Sources (per 100g cooked)
| Source | Protein | PDCAAS | Note |
|--------|---------|--------|------|
| Soy (tofu, tempeh) | 8-19g | 0.91 | Best plant complete protein |
| Quinoa | 4.4g | 0.81 | Also high in fiber |
| Buckwheat | 3.4g | 0.78 | Gluten-free grain |
| Hemp seeds | 31g | 0.63 | Also omega-3 rich |

## Complementary Combinations
These pairs together form complete proteins:
- **Rice + beans** (Latin American staple)
- **Pita + hummus** (chickpeas + wheat)
- **Peanut butter + bread** (legume + grain)
- **Lentil soup + rice** (Indian dal)
- **Corn tortilla + black beans** (Mexican staple)

Note: Complementary proteins don't need to be eaten in the same meal — within the same day is sufficient.
