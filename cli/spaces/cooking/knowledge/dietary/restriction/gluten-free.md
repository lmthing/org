# Gluten-Free Cooking

Gluten-free cooking avoids wheat, barley, rye, and products derived from them. This is essential for people with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity.

## Substitution Strategies
- **Flour** — Rice flour, almond flour, oat flour (certified GF), tapioca starch, potato starch, or commercial GF blends
- **Pasta** — Rice noodles, corn pasta, chickpea pasta, zucchini noodles, sweet potato glass noodles
- **Breadcrumbs** — Crushed rice crackers, almond meal, crushed cornflakes, polenta crumbs
- **Thickeners** — Cornstarch or arrowroot instead of wheat flour for sauces and gravies
- **Soy sauce** — Use tamari (check label for wheat-free) or coconut aminos

## Hidden Gluten Sources
- Soy sauce (most brands contain wheat)
- Malt vinegar
- Some spice blends and bouillon cubes
- Beer (use GF beer or wine for cooking)
- Processed foods with "modified food starch"

## Tips
- Many cuisines are naturally gluten-free: Mexican (corn-based), Japanese (rice-based), Indian (lentil and rice dishes)
- When baking GF, add xanthan gum (½ tsp per cup of flour) to improve structure
- Always check labels — gluten hides in unexpected places
