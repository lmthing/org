# Dairy-Free Cooking

Dairy-free cooking eliminates milk, cheese, butter, cream, yogurt, and all products containing them.

## Substitution Strategies
- **Butter** — Olive oil, coconut oil, or vegan butter. For baking, use coconut oil (solid) at a 1:1 ratio.
- **Milk** — Oat milk (best for cooking/baking), coconut milk (rich sauces, curries), almond milk (light uses)
- **Cream** — Full-fat coconut cream, cashew cream (blend soaked cashews with water)
- **Cheese** — Nutritional yeast for cheesy flavor, cashew-based sauces for creaminess, avocado for richness
- **Yogurt** — Coconut yogurt or soy yogurt

## Tips
- Coconut cream is the best dairy-free substitute for heavy cream in sauces — refrigerate the can and scoop the solid cream
- Nutritional yeast adds a savory, cheese-like flavor to pasta, popcorn, and sauces
- Many Asian cuisines (Thai, Chinese, Japanese) are naturally dairy-free and excellent references
- When making a roux, use olive oil or vegan butter instead of butter — the technique is identical
